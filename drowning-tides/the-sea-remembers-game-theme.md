# The Sea Remembers

### A Game Theme Document

---

## Concept

A blend of **Subnautica** and **DREDGE** — not by combining their mechanics, but by combining their emotional arcs.

| Subnautica | DREDGE |
|---|---|
| Wonder, exploration, survival, deep-sea mystery | Isolation, maritime folklore, cosmic horror, corruption |

**Core Theme: "The Sea Remembers"**

You are a lone mariner exploring an archipelago where the ocean is slowly revealing memories that should have remained buried.

---

## Day / Night Cycle

### During the Day

- Fishing
- Salvaging wrecks
- Mapping islands
- Gathering resources
- Meeting isolated coastal communities

### At Night

- The sea becomes psychologically unstable.
- Landmarks move.
- Constellations change.
- Creatures appear where they shouldn't.
- Memories from lost sailors manifest physically.

**The horror is never fully explained.**

---

## Visual Direction

### Cozy Surface World

Soft painterly skies. Low-poly fishing villages. Warm lantern light. Gentle waves. Hand-painted textures.

**Palette:**

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Seafoam Green | `#93C5B5` | Shallow water, foliage |
| Weathered Blue | `#6A8EA0` | Sky, distant ocean |
| Muted Orange | `#D4956A` | Sunset, lantern glow |
| Driftwood Brown | `#8B7355` | Wood, earth, docks |
| Warm White | `#F5ECD7` | Sails, foam, clouds |

### The Deep

As depth increases:

- Colors desaturate.
- Geometry becomes abstract.
- Rock formations resemble impossible architecture.
- Schools of fish move in unnatural patterns.
- Ancient structures appear partially organic.

**The deeper the player goes, the less the world obeys normal rules.**

| Depth | Hex | Feeling |
|---|---|---|
| Twilight Zone | `#2E4A62` | Unease, fading familiarity |
| Abyssal Layer | `#1A2A3A` | Isolation, pressure |
| The Forgotten | `#0D1117` | Dread, revelation |
| Bioluminescent Accent | `#4AEADC` | Life where none should exist |

## Boat Design Philosophy

The boat should feel like a character.

### Early Game

Small fishing vessel. Wooden hull. One lantern. Reliable and cozy.

> *The boat is your only constant. It creaks in a way that feels like breathing.*

### Mid Game

Reinforced hull. Diving equipment. Strange artifacts mounted onboard.

> *You tell yourself the modifications are upgrades. The hull groans differently now.*

### Late Game

The boat becomes visibly altered. Coral grows where wood should be. Lights pulse like living organs.

> *The player wonders whether upgrades are helping — or infecting the vessel.*

---

## Cosmic Horror Approach

**Avoid giant tentacle monsters as the primary horror.**

More unsettling ideas:

### The False Lighthouse

A lighthouse appears on the horizon.
Everyone in town insists it has never existed.
Yet every night it gets closer.

### The Silent Fleet

You discover abandoned fishing boats. Each contains:

- Fresh food
- Warm engines
- No crew

Nobody knows where the sailors went.

### The Deep Choir

At extreme depths, sonar returns musical notes instead of echoes.
The notes form a repeating melody.
Eventually the player can hum it.

---

## Gameplay Loop

```
1. Leave harbor.
2. Fish and explore.
3. Discover anomalies.
4. Dive for artifacts.
5. Return before darkness.
6. Upgrade vessel.
7. Uncover the mystery.
```

Simple but highly addictive.

---

## The Unique Twist

Most ocean horror games ask:

> *"What monster is down there?"*

This game asks:

> *"Why does the ocean seem to know who you are?"*

The sea isn't hiding creatures. **It's hiding memories.**

That creates a more psychological and impressionistic form of cosmic horror — one where the art gradually shifts from cozy maritime realism into dreamlike brushstrokes and impossible shapes as the player drifts farther from safety.
